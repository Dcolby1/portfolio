§ I  —  About

I'm a final-year double-major at the University of Tennessee, Knoxville, working at an intersection most people don't expect: applied artificial intelligence and history. Most of what I build involves large language models — a deployed full-stack PWA for shared movie recommendations, a RAG system for academic advising, an offline AI that runs on consumer hardware. But the historical training matters as much as the engineering does; it's what teaches me to ask better questions about what we're building, and why.

PROJECT  02
Working Prototype

Advising Vols

RAG-powered academic advising for the University of Tennessee.

A Retrieval-Augmented Generation system that lets students and academic advisors query UTK policy, course, and enrollment data in natural language — replacing manual lookups across scattered PDFs and portals. Includes a custom PDF parsing pipeline that ingests DARS (Degree Audit Reporting System) documents across multiple program formats, plus separate student- and advisor-facing portals. Built with a five-person team.

PROJECT  03
Completed

Offline AI System

Local LLM inference, no internet required.

A fully offline AI system that runs large language models locally on consumer hardware — no cloud, no API calls, no internet dependency. Implements a local RAG pipeline with ChromaDB for vector storage, supporting document-grounded question answering with low-latency private inference. Includes formal HCI documentation and Wizard of Oz usability testing.
